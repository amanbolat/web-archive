Verbs with the genitive – some examples

Some well-known verbs with genitive object

In this article about verbs in the genitive case, you'll learn more about some verbs with the genitive case . Below is a short list of verbs followed by a genitive object. These verbs can, however, be followed by an additional object (accusative or dative). Many of the verbs with a genitive object are reflexive verbs, whose reflexive pronouns are often in the accusative case.

 

accuse / accuse / suspect

The verbs ' anklagen ' and ' beschuldigen ' are verbs with the genitive case , in which a specific accusation is made against a person. The content of the accusation is in the genitive case .

The bank robber is charged with robbery .

The police accuse the bank robber  of robbery .

The police suspect the thief of theft .

require

When something is needed , we can also express this with the verb ‘ need ’, as here…

This text requires careful review .

 

lack

If something is missing or lacking, you can also express it with the verb ' ermangeln ' (to lack) in the genitive case. We can express what is "lacking" with the verb ' ermangeln ' (to lack) in the genitive case , as in this example...

His plan lacks their support .

 

commemorate

If we want to express that we are thinking about past events or deceased people, we can also use the verb ‘ gedenken ’ with these people or events in the genitive , as in the following example:

The literary association commemorates the writer with a reading from his books.

 

help yourself

When someone uses, utilizes, or employs something, they are making use of it. This thing is in the genitive case . Here's an example...

An accountant uses a computer program for his accounting. 

 

reflect

The verb ' sich besinnen ' is a reflexive verb whose reflexive pronoun is in the accusative case. Two variants of this verb are possible. The first variant is ' sich besinnen auf ' with the accusative case. This is a verb with the fixed preposition ' auf '.

The second variant is the verb ' sich besinnen ' with a genitive object that describes the object of reflection. Let's look at two examples of 'sich besinnen'...

The party is returning to its old values .

Here we see the verb ‘ sich besinnen auf ’ with the accusative.

The party is returning to its old values .

In this example the object is in the genitive .

 

abstain

Anyone who doesn't want to express their opinion, or who can't decide between one option and another, abstains. This reflexive verb is often used when it comes to voting or elections, as in this example...

Some members of the opposition abstained from voting .

 

to get rid of

If you no longer need something and don't want it anymore, you want to get rid of it or get rid of the thing (genitive) , as can be seen in this example...

Paul gets rid of his old computer .

 

enjoy

The reflexive verb ‘ sich fröhlichen ’ is a synonym for ‘to be glad about’, ‘to be pleased about’ , ‘to enjoy’ or ‘to be in possession of something’.

It can be used either as a verb with the fixed preposition ' an ' and a dative object , or without a preposition and using the genitive . The examples illustrate both possibilities...

The grandmother enjoys her grandchildren .

The meaning here is more 'to be happy about'.

The rock singer enjoys great popularity .

Here the verb means ' to delight ', ' to enjoy ' or 'to be in possession of' .

 

to remember

The reflexive verb ' sich erinnern ' can be used either as a verb with the fixed preposition 'an' and an accusative object , or without a preposition and with the genitive . The examples show both possibilities...

Mrs. Müller remembers the appointment with the boss .

Mrs. Müller remembers the meeting with the boss.

 

to be ashamed

The verb ' sich schämen ' is also a reflexive verb . You use it when you feel you've done something wrong or are embarrassed, as in this example.

The little boy is ashamed of his lies .
